Алексей В.Н.
Норка Алексей Владимирович 24.12.1989 - 24.12.2001

Причина смерти: Рак

Terrance Stanley Fox was a Canadian athlete, humanitarian, and cancer research activist. In 1980, having lost one leg to cancer, he set out on a cross-Canada run from east to west to raise funds and awareness for cancer research. Despite his cancer spreading and forcing him to conclude his journey after 143 days and 5,373 kilometers, ultimately leading to his demise, his endeavors left a lasting global legacy. The annual Terry Fox Run, initiated in 1981, has expanded to involve millions of participants across over 60 countries, making it the world's largest one-day fundraiser for cancer research, with over C$850 million raised in his honor as of September 2022. Fox, known for his distance running and basketball skills in his high school (now named after him) and Simon Fraser University, underwent the amputation of his right leg in 1977 due to osteosarcoma. Despite this, he continued running with a prosthetic leg and also excelled in wheelchair basketball, winning three national championships in Vancouver. In 1980, he initiated the Marathon of Hope, a coast-to-coast run aimed at raising funds for cancer research. His goal was to collect one dollar from each of Canada's 24 million people. Starting with little attention in St John's, Newfoundland and Labrador, in April, he ran the equivalent of a full marathon every day. Fox gained national acclaim by the time he reached Ontario, making numerous public appearances with businessmen, athletes, and politicians to raise funds. His run came to an end outside Thunder Bay when cancer spread to his lungs. His aspirations of overcoming the disease and completing his run were dashed when he passed away nine months later.
